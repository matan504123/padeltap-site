Frequently asked

How do I get the watch app installed?

If you've installed PadelTap on your iPhone and you have a paired Apple Watch, the watch app installs automatically within a minute or two. If it doesn't appear: open the Watch app on your iPhone → scroll to "Available Apps" → tap "Install" next to PadelTap.

How do I start scoring on the watch?

The watch home screen has one big Start button, with its label and behavior driven by your Default Match Type (set during onboarding or in watch Settings). Pick from Normal, Mixing, Training, or Americano. On the scoreboard: single tap for your team, double tap for the opponents, long-press to undo the last point.

What's the difference between the match types?

  • Normal — a full padel match. Standard game/set/match scoring with your chosen deuce rule (Star Point, Golden Point, or Advantage).
  • Mixing — for casual sessions with rotating partners. Each rally still uses standard game scoring, but games accumulate into a single running tally (no sets, no match winner). End it when you're done playing.
  • Training — workout only. Starts an Apple Health Tennis workout without any scoring UI. No taps to score; just hit start and end.
  • Americano — point-counting format. Each tap is one point, and a round ends when the total points reach a cap (default 32, configurable in watch Settings). Multiple rounds make up a session; the summary shows rounds won/lost and total points.

How do I change my Default Match Type or Americano point cap?

On the watch, open Settings → Default Match Type to pick the format the Start button uses. For Americano, scroll down in Settings to Americano → Points per round to change the round-point cap.

Why doesn't a map appear on my workout in Apple Health?

The map needs Location permission ("When in Use") granted to PadelTap and a clear-enough GPS fix at the start of the workout. Indoor courts may not get a usable fix. Without location, the workout still saves with heart rate, calories, and duration — just no map.

Why doesn't temperature / humidity show on my workout?

Weather requires both a valid GPS fix and an internet connection on the watch (via cellular or via Bluetooth-to-iPhone). If either is missing, the workout still saves; the weather row is just omitted.

How do I tag players on a match?

On your iPhone, open the History tab, tap a match, then tap each player slot. You can pick from your saved players or add a new one. Tagged player names do sync back to the watch — open the same match on your watch and you'll see the YOU / THEM block under the headline. Edits made while the watch is off your wrist queue up safely and arrive when you put it back on. Training and Americano sessions don't have player slots (training is workout-only; Americano partners rotate every round).

I tagged some matches before the latest update and the names aren't on the watch yet — can I backfill?

Yes. On the iPhone, open Settings tab → "Push player tags & venues to watch". That queues every match's enrichment back to the watch in one batch. The button shows a spinner and a confirmation alert. Payloads queue safely if the watch is offline.

How does the "days played" stat work?

On the iPhone Stats tab you'll see a "days played" total. You can seed the starting number in two places: during watch onboarding (a Stepper page asks "How many days have you played before this app?") or the first time you open the iPhone Stats tab. The value syncs across both devices automatically. From then on, every day you finish at least one match, mixing session, training, or Americano session counts as one more day. You can edit the baseline anytime in iPhone Settings → Padel Activity.

Can I rearrange the Stats tab?

Yes. Tap the controls icon in the top right of the Stats tab to open Customize Stats. Drag rows to reorder, toggle to hide sections. The four headline tiles at the top and the lifetime "days played" card stay pinned and always show first.

What is the Live tab on the iPhone?

An opt-in courtside scoreboard. When you enable it in iPhone Settings → Live court scoreboard, a Live tab appears between History and Stats. Place the iPhone sideways at the side of the court and it mirrors the watch in real time — big sets / games / current-game points so the score is readable from across the net. You can enable it per format (Normal / Mixing / Americano); Training never appears in Live. The iPhone screen stays awake while the Live tab is open, then resumes normal sleep behaviour when you switch tabs.

Can I flip the Live view so the orange (THEM) side is on the left?

Yes. Tap the arrow-swap button centered at the top of the Live view to swap the YOU and THEM cards — they slide across, and team colors travel with the team (blue YOU and orange THEM stay matched, the cards just change position). Useful when you're standing on the right side of the court and want the layout to mirror what you actually see.

Why are the big numbers showing "2" and "1" when no point has been played yet?

Between games — after one game ends and before the first point of the next — the big numbers show the current set's games count, so you read the set score at a glance instead of "0–0" points. As soon as the next point is scored, the display flips back to 15 / 30 / 40 / AD. The same rule applies to the watch scoreboard.

Why does one side blank out when the other has advantage?

When one team is at advantage (AD), the other side renders no number — just the colored card. From across the court the AD side is unambiguous, and on the watch the larger empty card makes it easier to read who's ahead at a glance.

How is the "Deuces" stat counted?

One count per game that reached 40-40 at least once, credited to whichever side won that game. Multi-cycle deuce battles still count as a single game — a game that swung through 40-40 three times before YOU finally won adds one to YOUR side of the Deuces card, not three. So if you had three games go to deuce in the range and you won two of them, the headline reads "3 games reached deuce · YOU 2 · THEM 1." The per-mode breakdown below splits the same totals by the rule the match was played under (Star Point / Golden Point / Advantage).

How do I make the iPhone read the score out loud?

Once the Live tab is on, scroll down in the same Settings section to find Voice. Toggle "Speak the score" on, pick a verbosity level (Full / Highlights / Milestones), and set the volume slider — release it to hear a preview. Announcements play through the iPhone speaker even when the silent switch is on, and lower the volume of any music playing. A small speaker icon on the Live view itself lets you mute on the fly without going back to Settings. English only for now. For a much more natural-sounding voice, install an Enhanced or Premium English voice from iOS Settings → Accessibility → Spoken Content → Voices — PadelTap picks the best-quality one available automatically.

Where do I see the map and weather for a match?

On your iPhone, open the History tab, tap any match — you'll see an embedded map centered on where you played, plus a Weather row in the Stats section (temperature + humidity captured at workout start). Tap the map to open the location in the Maps app. If a match has no map / weather, it usually means the watch didn't get a usable GPS fix at the time (indoor courts, location permission denied, etc.).

What are the heart-rate zones in the match detail?

The Heart-rate zones section shows how much time you spent in each of five effort bands (60% / 70% / 80% / 90% of your max HR) during the match. The first time you open it, the iPhone asks for HealthKit permission so it can read the workout's HR samples. Max HR is estimated from your age (220 − age) read from HealthKit; if you haven't set a date of birth there it falls back to age 30.

A match I played on my watch isn't showing on my iPhone

Watch-to-iPhone sync is automatic when both devices are nearby. If something looks missing, on the iPhone go to Settings tab → "Resync from watch". The sync is durable: even if the iPhone is off when you finish a match, it'll arrive once they're paired again.

The app crashes / behaves strangely

Force-quit PadelTap on both devices and reopen. If it persists, email a short description of what happened and what device(s) you're on. Mid-match crashes are recovered automatically: when you reopen the watch app, you'll be offered to resume the in-progress match.
