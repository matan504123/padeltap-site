Frequently asked

How do I get the watch app installed?

If you've installed PadelTap on your iPhone and you have a paired Apple Watch, the watch app installs automatically within a minute or two. If it doesn't appear: open the Watch app on your iPhone → scroll to "Available Apps" → tap "Install" next to PadelTap.

How do I start scoring on the watch?

The watch home screen has one big Start button, with its label and behavior driven by your Default Match Type (set during onboarding or in watch Settings). Pick from Normal, Mixing, Training, or Americano. On the scoreboard: single tap for your team, double tap for the opponents, long-press to undo the last point.

What's the difference between the match types?

  • Normal — a full padel match. Standard game/set/match scoring with your chosen deuce rule (Star Point, Golden Point, or Advantage).
  • Mixing — for casual sessions with rotating partners. Each rally still uses standard game scoring, but games accumulate into a single running tally (no sets, no match winner). End it when you're done playing.
  • Training — workout only. Starts an Apple Health Tennis workout without any scoring UI. No taps to score; just hit start and end.
  • Americano — point-counting format. Each tap is one point, and a round ends when the total points reach a cap (default 32, configurable in watch Settings). Multiple rounds make up a session; the summary shows rounds won/lost and total points.

How do I change my Default Match Type or Americano point cap?

On the watch, open Settings → Default Match Type to pick the format the Start button uses. For Americano, scroll down in Settings to Americano → Points per round to change the round-point cap.

Why doesn't a map appear on my workout in Apple Health?

The map needs Location permission ("When in Use") granted to PadelTap and a clear-enough GPS fix at the start of the workout. Indoor courts may not get a usable fix. Without location, the workout still saves with heart rate, calories, and duration — just no map.

Why doesn't temperature / humidity show on my workout?

Weather requires both a valid GPS fix and an internet connection on the watch (via cellular or via Bluetooth-to-iPhone). If either is missing, the workout still saves; the weather row is just omitted.

How do I tag players on a match?

On your iPhone, open the History tab, tap a match, then tap each player slot. You can pick from your saved players or add a new one. Players are stored on your iPhone only; they don't sync back to the watch. Training and Americano sessions don't have player slots (training is workout-only; Americano partners rotate every round).

How does the "days played" stat work?

On the iPhone Stats tab you'll see a "days played" total. The first time you open Stats, you can enter an estimate of how many days you played padel before installing PadelTap. From then on, every day you finish at least one match, mixing session, training, or Americano session counts as one more day automatically. You can edit the baseline anytime in iPhone Settings → Padel Activity.

Can I rearrange the Stats tab?

Yes. Tap the controls icon in the top right of the Stats tab to open Customize Stats. Drag rows to reorder, toggle to hide sections. The four headline tiles at the top and the lifetime "days played" card stay pinned and always show first.

A match I played on my watch isn't showing on my iPhone

Watch-to-iPhone sync is automatic when both devices are nearby. If something looks missing, on the iPhone go to Settings tab → "Resync from watch". The sync is durable: even if the iPhone is off when you finish a match, it'll arrive once they're paired again.

The app crashes / behaves strangely

Force-quit PadelTap on both devices and reopen. If it persists, email a short description of what happened and what device(s) you're on. Mid-match crashes are recovered automatically: when you reopen the watch app, you'll be offered to resume the in-progress match.
